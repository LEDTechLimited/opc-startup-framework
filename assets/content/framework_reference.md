## 1. The 7 Execution Layers

The framework's core architecture. Build **inside-out** — Layer 1 first, Layer 7 last. Skipping creates incoherent messaging and wasted spend.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Layer 7 — Sales Funnel & Retention                 │ ← Last
│  Layer 6 — Channel & Campaign Mix                   │
│  Layer 5 — Content & SEO/GEO                        │
│  Layer 4 — Website & Product UX                     │
│  Layer 3 — Visual & Design System                   │
│  Layer 2 — Positioning & ICP                        │
│  Layer 1 — Brand Foundation                         │ ← First (innermost)
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Layer 1 — Brand Foundation
**Locks**: Vision (your North Star), 5 Strategic Pillars, Anchor Decisions resolving open contradictions.
**Output**: A locked `master_strategy.md` that all subsequent layers must serve.
**Status when complete**: Versioned, dated, signed by founder. Amendments require explicit decision log.

### Layer 2 — Positioning & ICP
**Locks**: Tier-by-tier customer personas, Hero Product per tier, messaging matrix, competitor map.
**Critical depth**: First-principle drivers (the fear, the career risk, the underlying motivation) — not surface needs.
**Output deliverables**: `icp_personas.md`, `hero_product_mapping.md`, `messaging_matrix.md`, `competitor_mapping.md`.

### Layer 3 — Visual & Design System
**Locks**: Design principles, color system, typography, component library, photography style, motion principles.
**Constraint**: Must serve Layer 2 positioning, not impose its own aesthetic agenda.
**Output deliverables**: `design_principles.md`, `color_system.md`, `typography.md`, `component_library.md`, `brand_assets/`.

### Layer 4 — Website & Product UX
**Locks**: Information architecture, user journeys per tier, wireframes, AI interactions, CTA taxonomy.
**Common locked principles**: "Idiot-proof" hardware UX, Apple-style software simplicity, progressive disclosure.
**Output deliverables**: `information_architecture.md`, `user_journeys.md`, `wireframes/`, `ai_interactions.md`, `migration_plan.md`.

### Layer 5 — Content & SEO/GEO
**Locks**: Keyword matrix (including cold-intent keywords), GEO strategy (ChatGPT / Perplexity / Google AI Overview), content calendar, case study templates.
**Core strategy**: Cold keyword × N = niche demand. Filter every keyword by (1) clear solving intent (2) payment potential.
**Output deliverables**: `keyword_matrix.md`, `geo_strategy.md`, `content_calendar.md`, `case_study_template.md`.

### Layer 6 — Channel & Campaign Mix
**Locks**: Channel mix per tier, YouTube strategy, LinkedIn playbook, paid ads strategy, email funnel, PR outreach.
**Voice constraint**: Hooks must use practitioner-voice, not marketing-speak.
**Output deliverables**: `channel_mix.md`, `youtube_strategy.md`, `linkedin_playbook.md`, `paid_ads_strategy.md`, `email_funnel.md`.

### Layer 7 — Sales Funnel & Retention
**Locks**: Consultative sales flow, CRM setup, lead scoring, retention program, referral mechanism, customer success KPIs, crisis response protocol.
**Long-term play**: Retention quality determines whether you become the category-defining brand or just another vendor.
**Output deliverables**: `pro_consultative_flow.md`, `crm_setup.md`, `retention_program.md`, `referral_mechanism.md`, `customer_success_kpis.md`, `crisis_response_protocol.md`.

### Why inside-out matters

If you build Layer 5 (content) before Layer 2 (positioning), you write content for the wrong audience.
If you build Layer 6 (ads) before Layer 5 (content), your ads have nothing to point to.
If you build Layer 4 (UX) before Layer 3 (visual), you redo work twice.

The discipline: **lock the inner layer before opening the next**.

## 2. The Strategic Stack

Four documents form your strategic stack. Each plays a distinct role.

| Document | Defines | Status |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **Master Strategy** | WHAT your business IS — Vision + 5 Pillars + Anchor Decisions | Lock at v1.0, amend rarely |
| **OPC Operating Model** | HOW your business EVOLVES — Growth paths + Golden Triangle loop + Market antennas + Exit-readiness | Active, iterates quarterly |
| **Hormozi Offer & Money Model** | WHAT to SELL + HOW to SCALE — Starving crowd + Value equation + 4 profit levers + Sales as selection | Reviewed each tier launch |
| **Reference Prompts Library** | Tactical execution toolkit — 7-prompt framework for market validation, problem prioritization, offer design, distribution, viral content, competitor mapping, scale planning | Used per Layer kickoff |

### How they interact

```
Master Strategy (locked, authoritative)
         │
         ├── constrains ──► OPC Operating Model (iterates)
         │
         ├── constrains ──► Hormozi Money Model (reviewed)
         │
         └── filters    ──► Reference Prompts (tactical)
```

When tactical decisions conflict, **Master Strategy wins**. When Master Strategy needs change, version-bump and log the decision.

### The Master Strategy IS:
- Canonical strategic reference for all decisions
- The filter through which tactical execution is evaluated
- The anchor for resolving future ambiguity

### The Master Strategy IS NOT:
- A roadmap (timing/sequencing lives in execution plans)
- A diagnostic report (technical findings live in audit docs)
- A creative brief (specific copy/design lives in deliverable specs)
- A live document (changes require explicit decision log entry)

## 3. The Daily Filter

Every tactical decision passes through these 5 questions. If even one fails, redesign or kill the tactic.

> **1. Does this serve at least one of the 5 Pillars?**
> If not, you're scattering. Cut it.
>
> **2. Does this respect the Golden Triangle loop?**
> If a tactic optimizes one vertex but breaks the loop, it's a regression.
>
> **3. Does the copy pass the Concrete Proof Points test?**
> Specific protocols, specific numbers, specific clients. No "industry-leading" / "best-in-class" / "cutting-edge" fluff.
>
> **4. Does the offer pass the Value Equation test?**
> Maximize Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood. Minimize Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice.
>
> **5. Does this attract the right starving crowd?**
> Target a market with massive pain + purchasing power + reachability + growth. Not "everyone."

Print this filter. Pin it on your wall. Use it before launching anything.

## 4. The Golden Triangle Engine

The most important loop in the framework. **Wall's emphasis**: this is the engine, not the diagram.

### Structure

```
                    ┌────────────────────────┐
                    │   CORE VALUE (Top)      │
                    │   What makes your       │
                    │   category-defining     │
                    │   quality?              │
                    └────────────┬───────────┘
                                 │
                ┌────────────────┴────────────────┐
                ▼                                 ▼
   ┌────────────────────────┐         ┌────────────────────────┐
   │ PRODUCT FORM (Left)     │ ◄─────► │ USER NEEDS (Right)      │
   │ How you deliver value   │         │ What customers actually │
   │ — design principles,    │         │ want — technical,       │
   │ UX, hardware/software   │         │ experiential, custom    │
   │ form factor             │         │ requirements            │
   └────────────────────────┘         └────────────────────────┘
```

### The loop logic

```
User Needs evolve (e.g., new technical requirement emerges)
       ↓
Drives Product Form upgrade (you build the new capability)
       ↓
Reinforces Core Value (your category-defining quality strengthens)
       ↓
Wins more high-end customer feedback
       ↓
[loop back to User Needs]
```

### Quarterly Operating Cadence

Run this every quarter:

| Phase | Action | Owner |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **1. Listen** | Collect Pro tier unmet needs (sales calls, support tickets, forum monitoring) | Founder + AI agents |
| **2. Map** | Map each need to a Product Form gap | AI session |
| **3. Prioritize** | Rank by Willingness To Pay × Urgency | Founder decides |
| **4. Ship** | Engineer / build / write — produce a real Product Form change | Execution |
| **5. Measure** | Track reliability + satisfaction + retention metrics | Layer 7 metrics |
| **6. Recycle** | Convert success cases to marketing material (Layer 5 / 6) | AI repurposing |

### Why this is the engine

Without this loop, your 5 Pillars become stale. The loop ensures:
- Pillar 1 (Ecosystem) keeps evolving with real customer signal
- Pillar 5 (Concrete Proof Points) library refreshes with new wins
- Competitive moat deepens → category-defining positioning solidifies

If you only run this loop **annually**, you're too slow. **Monthly is fine for early stage**. **Quarterly is the steady state**.

## 5. OPC Operating Principles

How a One-Person Company actually evolves.

### 5.1 Three Growth Paths

#### Path 1 — Lifetime Value Enhancement
- **Strategy**: Refined service triggers secondary conversion
- **Tactic**: Customized solutions + value-added services
- **Application**: Each customer tier should have a natural upsell ladder
- **Example structure**: Hero Product → Add-on → License → Maintenance contract

#### Path 2 — AI-Driven Operations
- **Strategy**: AI compresses repetitive work
- **Founder time target**: 60-70% on creative / strategic / high-value work; 30-40% on system maintenance (handled by AI)
- **Implementation**: Identify daily repetitive tasks → automate with AI agents → free founder time for hard work

#### Path 3 — Niche Depth
- **Strategy**: Sub-segment messaging differentiation within each tier
- **Tactic**: Don't satisfy with "Pro / Standard / Entry" — go deeper into sub-niches with their own messaging
- **Referral psychology**: Use "loss aversion" — old customers who refer get a benefit they'd lose if they don't, while new customers don't feel they're getting a worse deal

### 5.2 Market Antenna Systems

How to detect demand signals others miss:

#### Cold Keyword Strategy
```
Rare keywords × N = Niche demand
```

Filter every cold keyword by:
- ✅ Clear solving intent (user is trying to fix a real problem)
- ✅ Payment potential (the problem is worth money)

If a keyword passes both, it's investable.

#### Customer Persona Depth — First Principles
Don't capture surface needs. Dig until you reach the underlying driver.

**Example**:
- Surface: "I want reliable equipment"
- First-principle: "If my next deployment fails, I lose the contract / get fired / damage my reputation"

Write messaging from the first-principle driver, not the surface need.

#### Kanban Feedback Management
```
[Received] → [Pending] → [Done / Discarded]
```

Public-facing version (selectively): Customer sees their suggestion enter Pending → moves to Done. Builds loyalty through transparency.

### 5.3 Exit-Readiness Principles

Even if you never sell, build as if you will. This forces hygiene.

#### Three Core Characteristics
1. **Independent operation** — Core processes don't depend on you personally; SOP'd; automated where possible
2. **Stable revenue** — Recurring or repeating customers; predictable cash flow; reasonable variance
3. **Transferable value** — Brand recognition, customer database, supplier relationships — all documented and inheritable

#### Three Implementation Directions
- **Process standardization** — SOPs replace tacit knowledge
- **Resource visibility** — All assets (customer DB, suppliers, content library) tracked and documented
- **Capability deposition** — Personal skills become system assets (e.g., founder's expertise → embedded in software / templates / training)

**Why this matters even pre-exit**: B2B Pro tier customers won't commit to "one-man-show" suppliers for major projects. Your category-defining positioning requires brand value to exist independently of you.

## 6. Hormozi Toolkit

Distilled from $100M Offers + $100M Money Models. Use these tools at offer-design time and pricing reviews.

### 6.1 Starving Crowd — 4 Filtering Criteria

Before building anything, validate the market passes all 4:

- **Massive pain** — The problem is severe enough to spend money to fix
- **Purchasing power** — The market has budget
- **Easy to reach** — Distribution channels exist
- **Growing market** — Tailwinds, not headwinds

Fail any one → reconsider the offer or the market.

### 6.2 The Value Equation

```
                    Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
   Value =   ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
```

**Maximize the numerator**:
- Dream Outcome: paint a clear picture of the after-state
- Perceived Likelihood: prove with evidence (case studies, guarantees, social proof)

**Minimize the denominator**:
- Time Delay: how fast does the customer get the result?
- Effort & Sacrifice: how much work / pain / change must they endure?

Run every offer through this equation before launch.

### 6.3 Building the Offer

1. **List every problem** the customer hits across the journey
2. **Design a solution** for each problem
3. **Decide delivery format** (digital, physical, service, hybrid)
4. **Bundle** into one irresistible package

### 6.4 Offer Enhancers

- **Bonuses** instead of discounts (preserves perceived value)
- **Scarcity & urgency** (real, not manufactured)
- **Risk reversal** through guarantees (asymmetric — make the seller's risk equal to or higher than the buyer's)
- **Naming formula**: Reason + Result + Time + Container
  - Example: "30-Day Conversion Optimization Sprint for Founders"

### 6.5 The 4 Profit Levers

To grow a business, only 4 levers exist:

1. **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** — Lower the cost to acquire
2. **LTV (Lifetime Value)** — Raise revenue per customer through retention + upsell
3. **Operational Leverage** — Scale revenue without proportional headcount
4. **Pricing Power** — Charge premium for differentiated solutions

If you're stuck, you're failing on one of these. Diagnose.

### 6.6 Sales as Selection (not Persuasion)

The mindset shift that separates premium operators from desperate ones:

- **Filter the right customers IN**
- **Filter the wrong customers OUT**
- **Diagnosis, not persuasion** — ask qualifying questions, surface fit
- **Asymmetric risk reversal** — your guarantee should make wrong-fit customers self-select away

A wrong-fit customer is more expensive than no customer (refunds, support burden, brand-damaging reviews).

### 6.7 Three Scaling Stages

Different bottlenecks at different revenue tiers:

| Stage | Revenue | Bottleneck | Focus |
|-------|---------|------------|-------|
| 1 | $0 → $1M | Product-market fit | Talk to users, iterate offer |
| 2 | $1M → $10M | Team + processes | Hire + standardize systems |
| 3 | $10M → $100M | Money model optimization | CFO-level pricing + capital structure |

Don't apply Stage 2 tactics at Stage 1.
